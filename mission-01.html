Family Mission 01

The Screenshot Trap

Screenshots feel like proof, but they can be fake, cropped, outdated, or missing the original source. This mission teaches kids to slow down before a screenshot turns into panic.

10-minute kid challenge 10-minute parent debrief Printable escape script

Kid challenge

Case File: Three Screenshots

Read each screenshot claim. Your job is not to decide whether it is impossible. Your job is to decide what you would need before trusting it or sharing it.

A

"Everyone is getting suspended tomorrow. Screenshot from the office."

No original post, no date, and a strong push to share fast.

Field check: Source + date missing.
B

"School update posted today. Here is the account and link."

Includes the original source, date, and a path to verify it.

Field check: Best evidence, but still verify the account.
C

"My friend says the principal sent this, but the top is cut off."

Could be real, but the screenshot hides who sent it and when.

Field check: Cropped context.

The four checks

Before you trust a screenshot, ask four things.

01

Source

Can I find the original account, message, article, or post?

02

Context

Is anything cropped out, blurred, summarized, or missing?

03

Date

Is this current, old, reposted, edited, or from a different place?

04

Pressure

Is someone trying to make me react before I can think?

Parent guide

Debrief script

Start with curiosity. The point is to build a habit your kid can use when you are not watching.

"What made this screenshot feel believable?"

"What would you need before forwarding it?"

"How could someone use this screenshot to rush people?"

"What is a calm sentence you could send instead of sharing it?"

Answer key

  • A is the least trustworthy because it has urgency without source, date, or context.
  • B is the strongest, but kids should still verify the account and link.
  • C is unresolved because the crop hides the sender and context.
  • The useful habit is slowing down, not becoming cynical about everything.

Phone note or printable card

If something online scares you

There is no online mistake so embarrassing that a kid should face it alone.

Kid script

  1. Stop replying.
  2. Do not send more.
  3. Do not pay.
  4. Save evidence.
  5. Tell one safe adult immediately.
  6. If you might hurt yourself, call or text 988 or go to an adult now.

Parent promise

"You are not in trouble right now. You did the right thing telling me. We will handle it together."
